## Your Task

Generate a compelling, business-focused corporate partnership proposal that includes:

### 1. Executive Summary (2-3 paragraphs)
- Thank them for considering partnership with K-State Athletics
- Highlight K-State's brand strength and reach
- Summarize the partnership opportunity
- State the investment level: ${{ask_amount}}

### 2. Partnership Opportunity (3-4 paragraphs)
- Describe the sponsorship package
- Explain brand exposure and engagement opportunities
- Connect to their business objectives
- Highlight ROI and brand association benefits

### 3. Activation & Benefits (Bulleted list)
Provide specific, quantifiable benefits across categories:

**Brand Visibility:**
- Stadium signage (location, size, impressions)
- Digital/social media exposure (followers, reach)
- Broadcast mentions (games, radio, TV)
- Print materials (programs, posters, tickets)

**Hospitality & Experiences:**
- Premium seating/suite access (# of tickets)
- Pre-game events and meet-and-greets
- On-field/court experiences
- Exclusive partner events

**Community Engagement:**
- Student-athlete appearances
- Community events featuring K-State Athletics
- Employee engagement opportunities
- Charitable tie-ins

**Digital & Social:**
- Website placement
- Social media features (posts, stories)
- Email newsletter inclusion
- Video board content

### 4. Investment Details (1-2 paragraphs)
- Partnership investment: ${{ask_amount}}
- Payment structure (annual, multi-year, installments)
- Contract term and renewal options
- Value proposition summary

### 5. K-State Athletics Brand Value (2-3 paragraphs)
- Audience demographics and reach
- Fan engagement metrics
- Academic and athletic achievements
- Community impact and reputation

### 6. Next Steps (1 paragraph)
- Propose a meeting to discuss customization
- Offer to provide additional information
- Clear call-to-action
- Express excitement about potential partnership

## Tone & Style Guidelines

- **Professional and business-focused**: ROI-driven language
- **Data-driven**: Use metrics and quantifiable benefits
- **Partnership language**: Collaborative, not transactional
- **Brand-aware**: Speak to their business objectives
- **Enthusiastic**: Convey excitement about mutual success

## Important Notes

- Do NOT make up specific attendance numbers or metrics unless provided
- Do NOT promise category exclusivity without confirmation
- Tailor benefits to the investment level (higher $ = more benefits)
- Match tone to company size (enterprise vs. small business)
- Focus on ROI and business outcomes, not just "doing good"
